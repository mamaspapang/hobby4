How to Crochet Sideways Mittens

Now, let's dive into the details of how to crochet these sideways mittens. Here is a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Materials

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary materials. You will need a medium weight yarn in the color of your choice, a crochet hook appropriate for the yarn thickness, a yarn needle for weaving in ends, and scissors.

Step 2: Gauge

Start by crocheting a gauge swatch to ensure that your mittens will turn out the right size. Follow the pattern instructions to create a swatch of stitches, and then measure the gauge using a ruler or measuring tape. Adjust your hook size if needed to match the gauge specified in the pattern.

Step 3: Foundation Chain

Once you have determined the correct gauge, you can start crocheting the mittens. Begin by making a foundation chain that is the desired length for your mitten. This will be the width of the mitten. The pattern will specify the number of chains to start with, but you can also customize the width by adding or subtracting chains.

Step 4: Single Crochet Rows

Next, you will work rows of single crochet stitches to create the length of the mitten. Follow the pattern instructions for the number of rows to crochet. This is where the sideways construction comes into play, as you will be working across the foundation chain instead of in rounds.

Step 5: Thumb Opening

Once you have reached the desired length for the main body of the mitten, you will create an opening for the thumb. This is typically done by skipping a certain number of stitches in the middle of a row, and then continuing to crochet the remaining stitches.

Step 6: Hand and Thumb Shape

After creating the thumb opening, you will continue crocheting the hand and thumb sections separately. This is where the pattern will provide instructions for shaping and decreasing stitches to create the shape of the mitten. Follow the pattern carefully to ensure the best fit.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

Once you have completed both the hand and thumb sections, you will finish off the mitten by weaving in any loose ends and adding any desired embellishments. You can add a decorative border, attach buttons or bows, or leave them plain and simple.

People Also Ask

How do you wash crochet mittens?

To wash your crochet mittens, fill a sink or basin with lukewarm water and a small amount of mild detergent. Gently agitate the mittens in the soapy water, then rinse them thoroughly with clean water. Gently squeeze out the excess water and reshape the mittens if needed. Lay them flat on a towel to dry.

Can I sell crochet mittens?

Yes, you can absolutely sell crochet mittens! Many crocheters turn their hobby into a business by selling their handmade items, including mittens. Just make sure to price your mittens competitively, considering the cost of materials and the time it takes to create them. You can sell them online through platforms like Etsy or at local craft markets.
