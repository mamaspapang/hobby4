Crocheting is a wonderful and relaxing craft, but sometimes those turning chains can be a bit bulky and create gaps in your work. That's where the alternative turning chain comes in handy. So, let's dive in and learn the secret to creating beautifully seamless crocheted pieces.

When it comes to crocheting, the turning chain is used to bring your yarn up to the height necessary for the next row. Traditionally, the turning chain is made by making chains with your hook. However, this can create gaps or bulkiness in your work, especially when you're working with taller stitches like double crochet or treble crochet. But fear not, my friend, there is a better way!

Creating the Perfect Alternative Turning Chain

Now, let me share with you the secret to crocheting the perfect alternative turning chain. Instead of making chains, you can use a technique called the "chainless starting stitch" or "standing stitch" to achieve a seamless look. Here's how it's done:

Step 1: Insert your hook into the first stitch of the row.

Start by inserting your crochet hook into the first stitch of the row, just like you normally would when starting a new row.

Step 2: Yarn over and pull up a loop.

Yarn over your hook, just as you would for a regular stitch, and then pull up a loop through the stitch. This will create the height needed for the next row.

Step 3: Continue with your desired stitch.

Now, you can just continue with your desired stitch. For example, if you're working with double crochet, yarn over and pull through two loops, then yarn over and pull through the remaining two loops on your hook. Repeat this process for each stitch in the row.

This alternative turning chain technique eliminates the gaps and bulkiness that can occur with traditional chain turning. It seamlessly blends in with your work, creating a more polished and professional finish.

Benefits and Advantages

The alternative turning chain technique offers several benefits and advantages. Here are a few of them:

  1. Seamless Appearance: By using the alternative turning chain, you can achieve a seamless and clean look in your crochet projects.
  2. Saves Yarn: Since you're skipping the traditional chain turning, you'll also save some yarn along the way.
  3. Reduces Bulk: The alternative turning chain helps to reduce any bulkiness that can occur with regular chain turning, resulting in a more comfortable and aesthetically pleasing finished product.
  4. Easy to Learn: Although it may take a little practice to get the hang of it, once you've mastered the technique, it becomes quite easy to implement.

In conclusion, the alternative turning chain is a game-changer in crochet. It allows you to create beautifully seamless and professional-looking pieces without any gaps or bulkiness. Give it a try in your next crochet project and see the difference it makes. Happy crocheting!
