Hey there! Today, I want to talk to you about how to do front post treble crochet and back post treble crochet, commonly abbreviated as fptr and bptr, respectively. Crocheting is such a wonderful hobby, and these advanced techniques can take your projects to the next level. So, let's dive in and explore the world of front post and back post treble crochet!

When it comes to fptr and bptr, it's all about the stitches. These techniques create a raised texture on the fabric, giving it a unique look and feel. Let's learn how to do them!

How to Do Front Post Treble Crochet (fptr)

The front post treble crochet stitch is a technique where you insert your hook from the front of your work to the back and then back to the front again around the post of the designated stitch. Here's how you do it:

  1. Start by yarn over twice.
  2. Next, locate the stitch that you want to work around.
  3. Insert your hook from the front to the back and then back to the front again around the post of the stitch.
  4. Yarn over and pull up a loop.
  5. Yarn over and pull through two loops on your hook.
  6. Yarn over and pull through the next two loops.
  7. Yarn over and pull through the remaining two loops.

And voila, you've successfully completed a front post treble crochet stitch!

How to Do Back Post Treble Crochet (bptr)

The back post treble crochet stitch is somewhat similar to the front post treble crochet, but instead, you insert your hook from the back of your work to the front and then back to the back again around the post of the designated stitch. Let's break it down:

  1. Begin by yarn over twice.
  2. Identify the stitch you wish to work around.
  3. Insert your hook from the back to the front and then back to the back again around the post of the stitch.
  4. Yarn over and pull up a loop.
  5. Yarn over and pull through two loops on your hook.
  6. Yarn over and pull through the next two loops.
  7. Yarn over and pull through the remaining two loops.

Amazing job! You've now mastered the back post treble crochet stitch!

Benefits and Advantages

Using front post treble crochet and back post treble crochet stitches can add incredible texture and depth to your crochet projects. Whether you're creating blankets, scarves, or garments, these stitches can make your items stand out.

Additionally, fptr and bptr stitches can be used to create beautiful ribbing or cabling effects in your crochet designs. They add an element of sophistication and intricacy that will impress anyone who sees your work!

The Conclusion

So, there you have it — a beginner-friendly guide to front post treble crochet and back post treble crochet. With a little practice, you'll be able to incorporate these stitches into your crocheting repertoire and create stunning pieces that showcase your skills. Get comfortable with the techniques, experiment with different patterns, and let your creativity soar!
